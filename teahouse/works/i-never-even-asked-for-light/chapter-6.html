Chapter Summary

A phone call ends up changing the course of Cartman and Butters' Very Exciting Scottish Adventure! for good.

Chapter Notes

I'm still alive.

Exhaustion is its own kind of anesthetic. Or well, at least to an extent.

After sleeping and, honestly, crying himself out on the bed sheets, Butters had crashed hard — perhaps not too literally, but he really needed some sleep, or at least some time to not focus on whatever the hell was happening for him. He had no dreams about monsters or angry friends, just black nothing. Which was, thankfully, a relief.

When he finally woke up, weak gray light was filtering through the cabin’s grimy windows. His clothes were still damp and gross, which sucked. He really, really should change soon, unless he wanted to catch pneumonia or something equally stupid.

Butters lay there for a while, staring at the water stains on the ceiling. Were they always there? He honestly couldn’t figure it out. There was also one thing he couldn’t easily figure out: if he’d always felt this empty or if it was a new development.

But his stomach eventually forced him out of bed. Moving felt like his joints had rusted over, but hunger was hunger. The cabin was quiet except for some muttering coming from the main room.

In the kitchen, he found Cartman sitting cross-legged on the floor, surrounded by their pathetic supplies. His fancy leather jacket was thrown aside, and he was hunched over his laptop, clicking away like a madman. Typical Cartman. The laptop screen showed one of last night’s photos — ugh. It was Butters in that ridiculous green suit, flailing around in the dark water like his life depended on it (to an extent, it did). But Cartman had been busy with what looked like a photo-editing program, adding a sickly green glow around the figure and some dramatic lens flare over the googly eyes. The water looked darker, and the mist, thicker. It was amateur hour, but Cartman seemed proud of his work.

What the hell was he doing.

He had his phone wedged between his ear and shoulder; he looked like he was aruging with someone on the phone.

“What do you mean it’s ‘not convincing’?” Cartman snarled into the phone. “It’s a fucking sea monster! Come on. What did you think it’d be made of, pudding? Look at the terror in its… in his, erm… in its eyes! That’s pure, primal fear! At least that’s what those bastards on NatGeo tell me. You can’t fake that!”

Some tinny, professional voice squawked back at him. Butters had no idea what that voice was saying, but it didn’t sound very good.

“No, I will not ‘submit it to a cryptozoology forum for peer review’. What the hell does that even mean!?” Cartman spat. “This is an exclusive! I’m offering your shitty tabloid the photo that will make you famous, and you’re talking about muh ‘pixel density’ and muh ‘digital manipulation’? That’s not kewl. I saw this creature myself and it almost bit me. That’s also not very kewl.”

Butters ignored the tantrum and made himself a sandwich. It wasn’t like he was going to gain anything by getting involved in Cartman’s arguments with whoever that was anyway. Back to his sandwich: it was just some bread and ham, hardly anything fancy, but when he took that first bite, it tasted like the best meal of his life. Considering the random crap he’d been eating since he got here, perhaps he wasn’t too far off from the truth.

He sat at the dusty kitchen table, eating and listening to Cartman’s business deal fall apart. He almost felt bad for him, honestly. The call, unsurprisingly, ended with Cartman screaming.

“Fine! I’ll take it to National Enquirer! They actually appreciate true journalism…. BITCH!” he yelled, before slamming his phone down so hard his screen might’ve actually cracked. “Goddamned liberals…”

Butters finished his sandwich and pulled out his own phone, surprised it still worked. To be fair, it was somewhat water-resistant… so that might’ve helped. His notifications were pretty much what he’d expected. Nothing from his parents, interestingly; they were probably enjoying the peace and quiet, thinking he was being properly supervised thousands of miles away. Heh. Out of sight, out of mind. There were also a few snaps from Clyde he hadn’t opened, and a day-old text from Jimmy asking for help with a punchline. That was it. No one else had even noticed he was gone.

Well… in that case…

He stared at Scott’s name in his contacts, then pressed “call”.

It took half a minute or so before Scott picked up.

“Butters? O-Oh, you’re alive. Uh… how’s it going there? You cool?”

“I’m fine, Scott,” Butters said, his voice flat. He felt weirdly detached, like he was watching his own life from somewhere far away. Something like that. “Well, besides the fact Cartman tried to sell fake photos of me in a monster suit to a tabloid. It didn’t work.”

There was a long sigh from the other end. “That’s… you know, I— actually no, I’m not even gonna ask what happened. But uh, you need to get out of there.”

“I know. It’s… complicated.” Butters took a breath. “Hey, Scott? Can I ask you something?”

“Anything.”

“What’s everyone else saying? About me, erm, about us being gone? Did you tell anyone?”

Scott hesitated. “Well… uh… heh, see. It’s… kind of a weird situation, Butters.”

“What do you mean, weird?”

“Weird weird.”

“For real?”

“For real real, not for play play. Look, I— well— I kind of… maybe… mentioned it to Kyle,” Scott admitted. At this point, his voice was so low it almost seemed like he was whispering. “I was worried, okay? I thought he should know. I didn’t think he’d…”

“Didn’t think he’d what?” Butters pressed.

Before Scott could answer, though, the kitchen door exploded open, slamming against the wall hard enough to splinter the frame. (Fortunately, that door stood still.) Cartman stood there, his face purple with rage and his eyes so wide it almost looked like they might fall out at any moment.

“Who the FUCK are you talking to!?” he roared.

Butters flinched, gripping the phone tighter. “I— uh— just Scott.”

“SCOTT?!” Cartman stomped forward and rapidly snatched the phone away from Butters. He hit speaker, pressing the button so hard that Butters almost thought the screen would creak. (He made a mental note to himself to get a better screen protector, just in case.) “You little diabetic bitch! Who did you tell?! Who knows we’re here?!”

Scott’s panicked voice filled the kitchen. He was fucked. “E-Eric! I… I was just worried about Butters!”

“I don’t give a flying FUCK about your worries!” Cartman screamed. He looked so angry that Butters ran out of dictionary words to describe his anger, and it’s not like he’d even tried. “I had a plan! A perfect, fool-fucking-proof plan, and you’re running your goddamn mouth all over town! WHO. KNOWS.”

“J-just Kyle!” Scott squeaked. “I only told Kyle!”

No.

“Kyle,” Cartman repeated. He was pissed, and it wasn’t hard to see that. “Oh, of course. Of course it was Kyle. How fantastic. And what did he do, huh? Let’s see… he called the cops? Well fuck them. Our parents? Fuck them too. What did that self-righteous piece of—”

“He’s… uh, well, he’s throwing a party.”

The statement was so unexpected it stopped Cartman’s rant cold. He stared at the phone, confused. “…a party?”

Butters leaned closer, equally bewildered. A party? Why would Kyle throw a party?

Scott, realizing he had no choice but to explain, took a shaky breath. “Yeah. A-at Tolkien’s house. I think his parents are out for the whole week a-and… he… he heard you two went to Scotland to hunt a monster, and he said… uh… that it the stupidest thing he’d ever heard and that you were both probably going to die.”

Oh, that made more sense.

It was painfully obvious to everyone who knew Kyle and Cartman that, in some way, they despised each other. Although they hung out a lot when younger, as they grew older, he recalled that Kyle tried many, many, many times to cut contact with Cartman — only for him to bawl his eyes out at him and his friends until they’d start talking again.

The idea made a lot of sense, thinking of it that way. With Cartman gone, he could finally gave some peace! If there was anything close enough to the devil, it was Eric Cartman himelf.

Cartman’s expression shifted from rage to what looked like confusion. “So he’s throwing a party because he thinks I’m gonna die? That’s… kinda sweet, actually. In a, uh, gay, obsessive way. Um, not that I—”

“That’s the thing, erm,” Scott said, his voice trembling. “The theme of the party… well, it’s celebrating quote unquote ‘what we hope is the imminent and painful demise of Eric Cartman’, or a least that’s what the text he sent us is reading.”

His face fell.

“But that’s not all,” Scott continued, even though his words tumbling out in a rush. “He… he felt bad for you, Butters. Well, at least I think he did. So yesterday we, erm— he did some sort of a memorial service.”

Butters blinked. “A… a what?”

“A memorial for you!” Scott squeaked. “Y-yeah. He said, and I quote, ‘We should honor the memory of our friend Butters, something something I forgot, something something was led to his doom by a sociopathic asshole.’ I forgot the rest of the quote, sorry. T-they got a keg, Clyde brought a Jell-O mold, and they printed out a big picture of you from school picture day to put over the fireplace. It wasn’t very recent, but uh, I-I guess it’s the intention that counts? It’s a week-long party, basically.”

A profound silence descended on the kitchen. Cartman stood frozen, the phone in his hand, his face cycling through disbelief, fury, and something that might have been hurt. He had no idea what the fuck was going on anymore. And Butters just sat there, trying to process the sheer absurdity of it all.

His friends. His friends were, at this very moment, drinking beer and eating fucking Jell-O at a party celebrating his death. This… made no sense at all. Well, he guessed it would make sense for Cartman, whom they never really liked, but… uh… did they actually think he, Butters, **was dead? Did they just pretend like he was gone… and didn’t even bother to ask if he was okay?

Jesus Christ.

Cartman stood in total silence for what seemed like 5 minutes, but was actually little under a minute. When he finally broke the silence, he from the phone to Butters, and oh boy, his eyes were burning with rage.

“They’re throwing a goddamned party,” he hissed. “While we’re out here… suffering for our art! For our, uh dream! All those losers and… and… and YOU!” He jabbed a finger at the phone. “ALL OF YOU! My so-called fucking friends!”

His voice began to rise. “You know what?! FUCK THIS! FUCK SCOTLAND! FUCK THAT BITCHASS MONSTER! AND FUCK ALL OF YOU!”

Uh-oh.

It didn’t take long for him to turn his blazing eyes on Butters. “This is YOUR fault! If you weren’t so goddamn pathetic and easy to convince, I would have found someone competent! Someone who wasn’t friends with a bunch of backstabbing, party-throwing ASSHOLES!”

For whatever reason, instead of just taking it, Butters felt something snap inside him. No, he wasn’t going to let Cartman act like this — not with Scott listening on the phone, for God’s sake. Being humiliated in person was already terrifying enough, but the fact he was in the Old World and not, say, 10 minutes away from Scott’s house would’ve made it a lot harder for him to, say, apologize for his so-called friend’s behavior. He stood up and let his chair scrape against the floor.

“You know what, Eric?” Butters said. His voice was shaking a bit, but he tried to not let that get the best of him. “You’re not some upstanding American like you think you are. You dragged me here! Y-you made me put on that stupid suit and jump in that freezing water! Fuck, you’ve been treating me like garbage this whole time!”

Cartman’s eyes widened in surprise. ”Oh, how cute. So NOW you grow a pair?!”

“Yeah! Maybe I am!” he shot back. “And-and maybe my friends are jerks for throwing a party, sure but at least they’re not trying to make money off my dead body!”

“You think you’re so innocent, don’t you?” Cartman stepped closer to him. “Well guess what, Butters? You’re not! You went along with this whole thing! You put on that goddamned suit, you jumped in that water, and you swam around like a fucking bastard. And if you think your precious friends are any better than me, then it’s about time you understood something: they all fucking suck! Even if I’m worse, they’re still pieces of shit who threw a party when they thought you were DEAD!”

Ouch. Butters felt the wind go out of his sails. Because, well… damn it, Cartman was right. About that part, anyway.

It wasn't that they didn't care about him, right? No, he was just collateral damage in one of Cartman’s failed plans. It was simple. The party was for Cartman, not him. But… why did it seem like they were more happy for Cartman being gone than sad for Butters being gone? Well, not that this was a bad thing, exactly — it’s just that Butters couldn’t understand why he seemed so unaware of everything going around him.

“We’re going home,” Cartman seethed, grabbing his leather jacket. “Pack your shit. This expedition is OVER. I’m going to go home and I am going to make them all PAY!”

He stormed out, presumably to pack his collection of dirty laundry and what was left of his shattered dreams. Butter’s phone, still on speaker, lay on the table. At least he didn’t break it…

“Butters? Are you there? Aw, jeez…”

Butters picked it up. “Yeah, Scott. Uh… I’m here.”

“Well, um, I’m so sorry, Butters, I didn’t mean for…”

“It’s okay,” Butters said, and he was surprised to find that he meant it. “It’s not your fault. I’ll deal with it.”

He ended the call and stood up. Well, a lot of things had just happened, and Butters’ five brain cells could barely keep up with whatever was going on. One day he was in his room, doing homework and complaining about the lamentable state of his friendships. The next he was freezing in a Scottish lake, living in a cabin in the middle of nowhere with Eric Cartman of all people, and finding out that, to an extent, his closest friends were treating him as if he were dead.

Just like Chewbacca living on Endor, it did not make sense.

Butters sighed. Well, if they were really going home, there were a couple of things he’d need to do. One of them was folding his few items of clothing and placing them back in his duffel bag. Another one was—

Huh. That smell. Hmm. Yeah… he’d need to take a shower. He was exhausted — more physically than mentally, and the day hadn’t even started! —, and plus, he also stank. Badly.

Yeah, a shower would be nice.
