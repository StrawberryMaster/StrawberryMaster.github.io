Chapter Summary

After aiding the nerds in another task, Pete and Jimmy discuss ways to get back at the jocks. Aided by Melvin, Gary thinks he may've found some new allies.

Oh boy, what a week.

Jimmy found out that getting the nerds to trust him was easier than it looked like — especially since they were fighting the same enemy. They had the guns, had the planning, had an army (or a lousy attempt at one), but lacked body strength; the weakest of the jocks would easily whoop all of them. But they needed someone to stand up for them — him, apparently. And as long as he did that, everything would be fine.

But Pete knew it was more complicated than that.

“Everybody knows who you are, but nobody knows who you are,” he thought, and it was true; he knew Jimmy was funny and insightful, even if at times he seemed like an airhead. But this was something he had a hard time translating to others, usually because he spent half of his time punching people and the other half banging them. It was his strength but also his weakness; for Gary, it was a walk in the park because apparently, he had so much freedom choosing who to talk to next that it allowed him to say whatever he wanted, and it stuck. Hell, even he didn’t know what was true about Jimmy and what wasn’t. It’s not like Jimmy wasn’t inaccessible — in fact, he was everywhere at this point — but he focused on the lesser things rather than the important ones first.

Knowing him, most of the rumors spread around were false — but there seemed to be a grain of truth to what he was saying. Sure, he didn’t think Jimmy was involved with barnyard animals (or whatever popped up in Gary’s insane mind), but he was pretty close to being called the Captain Hero of Bullworth. Whether ignoring the rumors — and even doubling down on some of them — was part of his strategy or something else was beyond him. But perhaps he was being too analytical, because his strategy was working. Needed some refinements here and there, sure, but it was working.

Later that evening, Jimmy and Pete sat in the dorm, tossing ideas back and forth about how to get back at the jocks. One of his latest adventures involved pranking the jocks at the funhouse in the Carnival on the other side of town; what he didn’t know is that it backfired and by the time he got there, both had been almost flattened by the jocks.

What Pete didn't know is that Jimmy too ended up flattened, and took about ~12 minutes to inflate again.

“How can someone be so disgusting?” Jimmy grumbled, still irritated. “Like, come on. He stank.”

This was true. But Pete attempted to get their minds working in sync as they plotted their next steps — so no distractions.

“Maybe we could mess with their gym equipment,” Jimmy suggested, leaning back in one of the poker table chairs. “Or we could sneak into the locker room and—”

Before he could finish, however, a familiar figure walked into the dorm. It was the devil himself, Gary. This wouldn’t be an issue if Pete ignored the last 6–8 months, which was tempting, but unfortunately impossible. What is he doing here?

How long had he been lingering there, listening to what they were saying? Probably not for long, and luckily they hadn’t mentioned him, but still. Gary leaned against the room’s doorframe, looking at the two of them with a mocking glint in his eyes.

“Well, well, well,” he drawled. “If it isn’t our very own lovebirds! Too bad for you, Petey; James here is too busy showing how much of a depraved human being he is to half this dorm.”

Jimmy rolled his eyes, visibly annoyed. He seemed irritated, but rather restrained too — at this point, they should’ve been fighting each other on the ground for the entire dorm to see. But that hadn’t happened. Kinda strange. “Cut it out, Gary. We’re trying to think here.”

And he chuckled, clearly enjoying himself. “Oh come on. Young love is such a… beautiful thing,” he teased, holding a hand to his heart dramatically. “I almost feel bad interrupting your little bonding session. Almost.”

Gary lingered for a moment longer, clearly savoring the discomfort he’d been causing. It was a classic Garyism; he’d always stick around unless sticking around wouldn’t benefit him anymore. Way before Jimmy showed up, several other “new kids” learned this the hard way. And few of them lasted long — some fell out of favor with Gary, others fell under relentless pressure from the other cliques at school. At this point, he wondered how Jimmy was still alive and standing.

Surprisingly, Jimmy had not reacted as badly as he thought it would — maybe because he was busy with other things, maybe because he didn’t wanna waste his time with him yet. And why would he? He had control of two of the school’s powerful cliques, was essentially domineering one and was getting closer to destroying another one. He didn’t want to run the school — but if that’s what it took to make everyone, including Gary, finally listen to him for once, then so be it, right?

In the corner, Gary spent about five minutes trying to mock Jimmy’s mannerisms, of which three minutes were spent replicating sex moves that he — as in Jimmy — supposedly knew. Though not a mimic at heart, he was clearly having fun, even if his attempts were mostly for his own amusement. Pete barely reacted, just watching with a tired expression — one that only seemed to fuel his antics. After repeated tries at trying to get their attention — and barely getting Pete’s — he shrugged, pushed off the doorframe and sauntered out of the room, still grinning like he’d just won a prize.

What the hell just happened.

Jimmy watched him go, a small frown on his face. “That guy is seriously messed up,” he muttered under his breath.

“Tell me about it.” Pete shook his head. “A-anyway, uh, where were we again? Wait.”

But before they could dive back into their scheming, Pete’s mind wandered again. A thought struck him like a bolt of lightning, and his eyes lit up as he turned to Jimmy. “Okay, I think I got it. Remember when you beat Bif last year? The whole thing shifted after that. You need to hit their strongest soldier again.”

“Hit Bif again?”

“N-no, no, I mean the jocks’ strongest soldier.”

“You mean Tad?” Jimmy asked.

“Not their leader. But someone who’s also big. Someone like Damon — he’s as dumb as a brick but just as tough.”

Jimmy raised an eyebrow. “You think I should just beat him?”

“Not physically,” Pete said, warming up to his idea. “What if we get him in trouble with Crabblesnitch? You know how much the principal loves that stupid paddle of his. We could pin something on Damon, make it look like he stole it.”

Luckily for him, this was enough to capture Jimmy’s interest in his plan. Bingo. “You mean, make it look like Damon messed with the paddle or something?”

“Exactly,” Pete continued, the plan forming in his head as he spoke. “We sneak into Crabblesnitch’s office, swipe the paddle, and then plant something of Damon’s in the office or whatever — or put the paddle in a place that only he could access, like his locker. We’ll make it look like he stole the paddle and get him in trouble.”

A grin slowly spread across Jimmy’s face. “Huh. That could actually work. And it would definitely piss off those dickheads.”

Nice job, Pete. If only Jimmy could think of these ideas by himself… but he was glad to help anyway. Suddenly energized, Jimmy decided to put the plan into action immediately. He sat up, grabbed his jacket and headed for the door. “I’m doing this now,” he said, his tone final. That was fast.

Pete hesitated, wanting to come along, but Jimmy was already halfway out the door. “Wait, I—” But it was too late. He was gone, leaving Pete alone in the room. When he got up and wandered into the corridor, hoping to catch up with Jimmy, he found… nothing. The hallway was empty.

He really was fast. But once again, little Pete was missing out on yet another adventure. He sighed. Shit. Not this again.

Above him, on the second floor, Gary leaned over the banister, watching Pete with a rather confused face. If he knew the exciting moments in the dorm were all like this, he’d have gone back to his pills and zoned out for the rest of the year. But if Jimmy couldn’t make his closest of friends happy, then that made things much easier for him…

“Oh yeah, I’m Jimmy Hopkins and I’m like Moses, only keeping it real. Blah blah blah give me money or give me hoes”-type shit. What did Gary even see in him? He should’ve taken care of him much, much earlier, like he did with a few others in his way. Once is luck — in his case bad luck —, twice is coincidence, thrice is a pattern; James was dumb but ruthless, and it was too late for him to turn back time.

He shrugged. “It’s a living,” before disappearing back to the second floor bathroom. Maybe Pete was right; maybe he should shower more often.

Pete didn’t hear much from Jimmy during that night.

Hours later, when the dorm was quiet and most of the students — him included — were asleep, the door to his room opened. Gary stumbled in, luckily not reeking of sweat but of something that smelled a lot like alcohol. His steps were rather unsteady, and his iPod was blaring a rough, unreleased Weezer demo. It was pretty good, but rather unwarranted at this time of night. When did Gary ever like Weezer? The distorted guitars and raw vocals were loud enough to wake him up.

“I got the best intel tonight,” Gary announced, nearly tripping over his own feet as he made his way toward his bed. “Melvin... that nerd’s feeding me everything. He knows all about James’s little encounters with one of those meathead jocks. It’s brilliant, Petey. Kinda sad too. But fucking brilliant.”

Pete, who had been lying in bed and staring at the ceiling, turned his head to look at Gary. Understandably frustrated, partially because he hated seeing Gary like this and partially because he wanted to sleep well that night, he sighed. “Gary, just turn it off, okay?” he said, his voice low and tired.

He blinked, as if he hadn’t fully processed what Pete said, then fumbled with his iPod until the music cut out. The room, naturally, fell into silence. At times, seeing these kinds of situations kept him up at night, both literally and figuratively; there was a rather deep, aching worry that gnawed at Pete whenever his roommate was like this. While he couldn’t care less about his interactions with the other students — even though Melvin was far from the most trusted person out there — he wondered why he couldn’t have been hanging out with someone else, at another time, and not right when he was sleeping.

Sometimes, it was less about how he acted; it was about how much worse it seemed to be getting, especially since he was in the dorm to see his behavior again.

“Goodnight, Gary,” Pete said, hoping to end the conversation there. He needed sleep, and so did Gary, even if he’d never admit it.

“Night.”

Of course, he wasn’t going to sleep then. Unlike party poopers like Pete, he was still taking his time planning things — even though these things were taking a while. His thoughts wandered back to the conversation he’d had with Melvin earlier. Melvin was definitely smarter than the other nerds, even though some of his ideas were completely ridiculous.

“You should… snort get some help from those delinquents from Blue Skies. They know what chaos is — they’ll show Jim what it’s like,” he said at the time.

“The dropouts? And you think you’re gonna help me with that?”

“Fo’ shizzle… maybe you could get your loverboy to do the connection, too!”

“I’m gonna kick your ass.”

But perhaps not all ideas. Gary had never backed down from a challenge — excluding those he knew he wouldn’t win. But this one wouldn’t be a bad idea. He hated the third rail of Bullworth as much as anyone else; not even the toughest of greasers hung out with the townies, and he honestly didn’t recall knowing someone who was savvy enough to make a deal with them. No one would do it, and there was no point in doing so.

Except that maybe there now was. And the best thing is that the person making this deal would be him.
