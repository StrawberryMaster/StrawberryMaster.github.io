Chapter Summary

It's Halloween.

Chapter Notes

Included here is cut content from the famous Halloween mission. This is probably not the last time you'll see cut content appearing here, but probably is the last time you'll see lots of dialogue of it being shown here.

Pete, with his face buried in one of the boys’ dorm trashcans, felt his last shred of dignity curdle and die. It was over. “I’m not owned! I’m not owned!” he continued to insist as he slowly shrank and transformed into a corncob — or that’s what his brain wanted him to think. He knew escape was just a pipe dream — the bunny costume transformed him into a giant, floppy target. The fact his voice was muffled by a crumpled napkin did not help.

He thought this year’s Halloween would be better, but it started off on the wrong foot. Part of him wanted to wallop Gary for even suggesting such an outfit to him. Part of him wanted to sleep forever and never wake up.

He was about to give up and accept his new role as Bullworth’s trashcan mascot when a rather bony hand latched onto his collar and hauled him out of the garbage. Pete stumbled back, blinking against the dim dorm light, and saw Jimmy standing there, decked out in a skeleton costume that honestly, actually looked decent — with the black fabric hugging his frame and his white bones painted on with surprising care. Very well-tailored. In his gloved hand, he clutched what could only be described as a particularly disgruntled-looking rat.

“Took you long enough,” Pete croaked, spitting out a soggy french fry that’d lodged itself in his cheek.

Jimmy just shrugged.

“I don’t know about you, Petey, but I’ve never eaten from a trash can…”

Before he could answer, another figure burst onto the scene. It was Gary, his usual attire replaced by a grotesquely authentic-looking German WWII uniform. His jaw dropped. Where did he even get that? On the other hand, considering there were seemingly no prefects around, it all started to make sense in his head.

“Come on, we have plans,” Gary said, with a wild glint in his eye.

Pete staggered to his feet, grateful for Jimmy’s outstretched hand. “This is insane,” he muttered, but he followed nonetheless.

This is when he found out Gary had planned a series of increasingly bizarre pranks — something which was definitely a Gary thing to do, but was caught off guard by it. Their first target: the pristine gates of the girls’ dorm. They noticed a few of the girls are chatting; he could see Eunice chatting with Angie, both with their own outfits. They weren’t bad outfits, too; Eunice was dressed as a princess and Angie as a witch. He’d be lying if he didn’t find it cute. Farther away from the doom gates was what looked like Mandy, pom-poms dangling as she waved her arms, talking to some girl Pete couldn’t place from this far off.

Gary, naturally, barked orders at Jimmy. “There,” he jabbed a finger toward the girls. “Throw the rat at the fat girl.”

Pete raised an eyebrow. “Gary, you’re one disturbed individual.”

“Shut up, bitch.”

Jimmy hesitated only a moment before complying, the rat flying through the air and landing with a squeal at Eunice’s feet.

“Oh my God, what is that!?” she screamed before realizing the thing that fell on her was, to no one’s surprise, a rat. Bad news for everyone’s eardrums, as she and Angie’s screams were so loud that they probably pierced the entirety of Bullworth and ran around Earth at least seven times.

Gary laughed. “Well done.”

And Pete had to admit, it was a bit funny. Not very original, maybe, but pretty damn funny. It was funnier considering they were all wearing all sorts of costumes, from the crappiest to the most detailed ones, and if they had a chance to ruin someone’s night, well, why not bet on it?

For the second prank, their target was Edna, the perpetually grumpy and infamous lunch lady. Gary held up a brightly colored candy with a rather cocky grin. “That’s the joke candy I got. Let’s go see if Edna’s hungry.”

The main building wasn’t really locked at night, but was heavily patrolled and that’s why getting in to retrieve something (or, for most people, do something) was almost impossible. But it was so weird when they entered it because it almost looked abandoned, most lights turned off except for a few in the cafeteria and some lights upstairs, where he assumed the teachers or prefects were at. At times, Pete could barely see the linoleum floor he was stepping on. As they made their way to the cafeteria, Gary turned to Jimmy, handing him the candy. “Pete, wait back here with me. You go feed the candy to that monster.”

Jimmy took the candy and nodded, sneaking towards Edna, who was grumbling over a pot of suspiciously gray stew. As he approached, Pete and Gary lingered near the entrance, peering around a corner. That costume's fur was beginning to itch against his neck.

Pete couldn’t resist a question that had been nagging him. “How is Edna still here, anyway?”

Gary snickered. “The food at the teacher’s party in the lounge has to come from somewhere, you know.”

True, true. He shifted uncomfortably in his bunny costume. “At least she doesn’t have to see me like… this. I look like a jerk.”

“No, you look fine. Don’t be a baby,” Gary retorted, his gaze lingering on the slightly askew pink fur. God, that was the best costume he could’ve gotten for him. If only he complained a little less…

“No, no, I look like a jerk,” Pete insisted.

“Just stop whining.” Gary reached out and adjusted Pete’s floppy ears, giving them a slight twist. “There. Better?”

There was an attempt.

From their vantage point, they watched Jimmy approach Edna, who was busy restocking the vending machines. Pete held his breath as Jimmy, with a practiced sleight of hand, slipped the joke candy amongst a pile of other, irrelevant ingredients.

“Oh, look!” she exclaimed. “I found a candy still wrapped! How lucky!”

Edna, unsuspecting, popped the candy into her mouth and chewed with a satisfied grunt. A few seconds later, her face contorted in disgust.

“Ugh,” she sputtered, spitting out the offending candy into a nearby trash can. “I got a little in the special punch! Oh well, a little Edna never hurt anyone!”

Pete could barely contain a snort. “There’s nothing little about Edna,” Gary muttered under his breath.

For their third prank, all Gary told them was that it “involved a little pyrotechnics.” And as they made their way back through the darkened main building, Gary suddenly halted.

“Hang on a second, I gotta get something,” Gary said, darting into one of the hallways.

Pete and Jimmy waited, the seconds stretching into a rather uneasy silence. What was he doing this time? He was probably gonna retrieve something from his locker, but what, exactly? When he emerged moments later, he carried a black bag with something the shape of a trapezoid.

“Let’s go!” he urged.

“What did you just get?” Pete asked, trying to peer into Gary’s hands.

“I’ll tell you later, let’s go!”

After leaving the main building (and luckily not being caught by Edna, who apparently didn’t spot them), they moved swiftly to the school’s parking lot. When they reached a secluded spot, hidden behind one of the walls, Gary finally pulled out the Volcano 4000, its label gleaming ominously in the dim light.

Pete’s eyes widened. “Are you serious?”

“Dead serious,” Gary grinned. “It’s Halloween, why wouldn’t I be serious? This is gonna be epic.”

The Volcano 4000 was a bit of an experiment gone wrong; after one of their members defected to the jocks, the nerds put Bucky — which Gary called the dumbest of them — in charge of making a new ploy to use against them in a future war. Reportedly 100 of these were made, but production was stopped when the observatory was mysteriously invaded one night — resulting in 80 of them being spread throughout the school. Most were MIA, but it just so happened that Gary had one of them. Huh.

“Well, those are our victims,” he declared, pointing at the bullies — Davis, Tom, and Trent. They were loitering, laughing and pushing each other around, luckily with no sign of Russell. Unsurprisingly, none of them were wearing any costumes, assuming Tom’s ephemeral black eye didn’t count.

“You’re up, James.”

Jimmy took the firework from Gary, and with a determined nod, he crept towards the bullies, the volcano-thingamajig clutched tightly in his hand. As he approached the group, he found a discreet spot to set up the firework, lighting up the fuse and rapidly retreating, the hiss of the igniting firework barely audible over the other sounds coming from the campus.

And then seconds later, the Volcano 4000 erupted, a dazzling explosion of light and sound that sent the bullies scattering in every direction. Ouch.

“OW!” one of them — Davis? — screamed. “I think I’m blind!”

Gary doubled over with laughter, tears streaming down his face. “Hahaha, nice one, Jimmy!”

“Let’s get out of here while they’re still stunned.”

Turns out the Volcano 4000’s effects were rather brief — good, since he assumed the nerds weren’t actually trying to kill people with these — and the trio scrambled to find hiding spots as the bullies angrily searched for the culprits. Gary and Jimmy quickly ducked behind a nearby dumpster, but Pete was left standing in the open, panicking as the bullies closed in.

“Sorry Pete, there’s no room back here,” Gary whispered, his eyes focused on Jimmy. Jesus, how come he was stinking already?

“But, but…” Pete stammered, looking around desperately.

Before he could find a hiding spot, one of the bullies spotted him and, unsurprisingly, delivered a swift kick to his groin.

“Ah-h-h, my balls! Ah-h-h-h!” Pete groaned, doubling over in pain. His dreams of being a father were now delayed by at least 5 years.

He vaguely saw Gary peek out from behind the dumpster, a mixture of amusement and horror flashing across his face.

“Wow. Thanks for taking one for the team, Pete,” he called out. “You’re such a trooper.”

Pete wanted to scream, but all that emerged was a strangled whimper. The pain was excruciating, making him wish he’d been a victim of the Volcano 4000 instead.

“Why didn’t you guys help me?!” he finally managed to gasp, his voice strained.

Gary shrugged, his voice barely a whisper. “Sorry, Pete, there’s no room back here. You wouldn’t have fit anyway. Besides, I didn’t want to ruin my costume.”

His heart sank, but he continued. For their third prank, they’d need to put a “Kick Me!” note on a nerd’s back, which was relatively simple. Their chosen victim was, no surprise here, Algie. After distracting some of the jocks with another one of Gary’s heavy machinery — some funny-looking fireworks — he got what he deserved.

For their fourth prank, however, he had something different in mind. During their pranks, Gary noticed Mr. Burton and Dr. Slawter, their biology teacher, were near the gym building, chatting — he wondered why they weren’t at the teacher’s party, but he assumed they were just waiting for the food to show up. Jimmy was entrusted with throwing itching powder at them… and guess who would be the one distracting the two.

“Pete, go distract them so Jimmy can hit them with the itch. We need some sort of distraction.”

“Awww, man!”

Pete, still smarting from the betrayal in the parking lot, took a deep breath before shuffling towards the two teachers.

“Uhh… hey, Mr. Burton,” he stammered, forcing a smile. “W-where’s your costume?”

Mr. Burton glanced down at his gym shorts. “I’m wearing it under these shorts, Kowalski,” he replied curtly.

“Oh.”

Dr. Slawter chuckled a bit. “What’s your costume?”

Pete felt his face redden. “Umm… I’m a giant pink bunny,” he admitted sheepishly.

The laughter came again, this time a chorus between the two teachers. Mr. Burton, however, quickly cleared his throat. “Child, you are pathetic!”

Slawter laughed, the sound cold and mocking. “Haha… ha… that’s so sad. Ha…”

“It uhh… it was Gary’s idea,” Pete mumbled, his voice barely above a whisper.

This seemed to ignite another spark in Mr. Burton’s already volatile mood. “So you have another boy dress you, do you?” he boomed, his voice echoing across the courtyard.

But Slawter just shrugged. “Well, if that’s what you like, who am I to question it?”

“That’s nonsense, Slawter!” Mr. Burton roared, his face turning a shade of purple rivaling Pete’s costume. “How will the children grow into upstanding members of society if we let them run rampant?! That’s socialism.”

“You make a good point, Burton,” he conceded, nodding.

Seizing the opportunity, Pete started to back away, hoping to escape their cruel taunts. “I think I’ll be going now,” he mumbled, the humiliation burning in his cheeks.

Mr. Burton, however, wasn’t finished. “I’ll be seeing you in the gym class, thumper,” he sneered. “And I’ll be sure everyone will have a good laugh at your costume!”

“P-please don’t make fun of me in front of everyone in class!” he pleaded, desperation creeping into his voice. But it was to no avail. Mr. Burton’s thin lips stretched into a cruel smile.

“You should’ve thought about that before you put on that wimpy furry costume, shouldn’t ya, bunny boy?”

Just then, from behind the hedge, a puff of white dust filled the air. Jimmy, to his relief, had finally unleashed the itching powder. Burton and Slawter, caught unaware, coughed and sputtered, their attention momentarily diverted from Pete.

And when the three realized this, Mr. Burton swatted at his arms and legs like a man possessed. “It itches!” he shrieked, his voice high-pitched with panic. “It itches like Athlete’s foot all over, aagh!” Slawter, initially confused by the prickly sensation, soon joined the scratching frenzy. “This feels nice… prickly, no! Wait… duaghhh!” he sputtered, his eyes watering.

But his moment of victory was short-lived. Mr. Burton, momentarily forgetting the itch, spun around, his gaze landing on the bunny ears bobbing above the hedge.

“You think this is funny, bunny boy?! I’ll make you pay in class.”

Slawter, recovering slightly, looked at Pete with disdain. “Well, Pete, I hope you found it funny because I’m sure the headmaster won’t.”

Pete groaned inwardly. “Aw, man!”

This was the worst thing he could ask for.

Slawter, noticing his dejection, looked at Burton. “Let us go, I heard Edna’s made special punch for the staff room.”

As they left, Pete felt a wave of nausea. Feeling utterly defeated, he glanced back at Gary and Jimmy, who were still grinning from their hiding spot. “I hate you guys! My mom’s gonna be so angry when she finds out.”

Gary shrugged nonchalantly. “Relax, Petey. It’s all part of the game.”

Very few could appreciate Pete’s drip that night.

Finally, for their final prank, Gary took them to the front of the Harrington House, the preps’ hangout in the school. The place was rather ugly outside, but was still better than almost anything on campus; especially inside. He wasn’t sure what was gonna happen now, though.

“Okay, here’s the deal…. we’re gonna feed Chad’s dog some of this rancid meat,” Gary explained, holding up a dubious-looking piece of meat, that was conveniently placed near the front doors.

Pete was livid.

“Wh-what the fuck?! I’ve been kicked in the balls, reported to the principal, and now you guys are messing around with dog crap?! I’m outta here!”

Gary waved him off dismissively. “Whatever, Pete. Okay, let’s do this, Jim…”

Feeling utterly defeated, Pete decided he’d had enough for one night. He slipped away from the group, his mind set on escaping the madness. Every step felt heavier than the last, the cold air nipping at his sweat-damp skin where the costume had chafed. He stank, and it was terrible. After entering his room, he immediately began peeling off the ridiculous bunny costume, tossing it into the corner.

“Ugh.” he sighed deeply, his body aching from the various indignities he’d endured throughout the evening. The only thing he could think of was taking a long, hot bath and then crashing into bed, hoping to forget the night’s events.

The bathroom down the hall was empty, thank God. The place was a mess and in need of care, and due to budget cuts, it had gone on without maintenance for years now. How everything still worked was a surprise to him. He twisted the shower knob all the way to the left, and the pipes groaned before spitting out a weak stream that slowly turned scalding. Steam soon curled up, fogging the cracked mirror just in front of him. Pete stepped in, wincing as the heat hit his skin — it was too hot, unfortunately, but he didn’t care; he just wanted to be clean. The water pounded his shoulders, stinging where the costume’s seams had rubbed him raw, and he tipped his head back, letting it soak his hair and run down his face. Being here was better than the previous 15 minutes he’d spent outside.

He grabbed the only bar of soap available to him, a chipped thing that smelled vaguely of pine, and scrubbed quite hard, working up a lather across his chest, under his arms, everywhere that damn costume had clung. Pete didn’t even wanna think about it ever again. Soon the water turned grayish at his feet, washing away the sweat (and part of his humiliation). He tried his best to keep his mind from replaying the events of the night, the bad ones at least. What a disaster. He rinsed off, the soap suds pooling around the rusty drain, and cranked the knob until the water cut off with a shudder.

Stepping out, he snagged a towel from the rack — that one was his, thankfully — and scrubbed it over his hair, then wrapped it around his waist. The mirror was too steamed up to see his reflection, which was fine; he didn’t want to look at himself anyway. Back in his room, he yanked open the top drawer, fishing out the first things his hands landed on: a gray T-shirt and a pair of plaid shorts that’d seen better days. Good enough. He just tugged them on and flopped onto his bed.

Gary could stumble in whenever; Pete just hoped sleep would hit first.

A while had passed since the events of Halloween. The days at Bullworth had returned to their usual madness, but Pete couldn’t shake off the lingering unease from the pranks and Gary’s relentless antics. He found himself sitting on the common room couch, fidgeting with his hands. The dorm was buzzing with the usual chatter and noise, but Pete’s mind was elsewhere; he really needed a break from everything going on.

At one point, Constantinos — one of the school’s more cynical students — walked into the room. God, he was so fucking annoying. He was like if Sheldon and Algie had a kid, just downright insufferable. Maybe Gary was right about him after all. He carried a stack of colorful tickets and began advertising loudly, “Fight night in the Hole!” he bellowed. “Get your tickets for the biggest brawl of the year! You don’t want to miss it!”

Pete perked up at the announcement. The Hole was infamous — a hidden, huge ring where the most fights took place. The last fight that happened there — two and a half months ago — sent one of the preps (Bryce?) spiraling to the floor. Thrilling, naturally, but also terrifying.

He approached Pete, a sly smile on his face. “Hey, Petey, got something special for you.” He handed Pete a ticket. “Courtesy of the boss.”

“Huh, thanks.”

He took the ticket, and glanced down at it, his eyes widening in surprise and fear as he read the details.

Jimmy Hopkins vs. Russell Northrop.

Jimmy and Russell. The new kid against the leader of the bullies. Pete’s mind raced. Why did Jimmy agree to a fight? What was his endgame? Sure, he knew he was trying to take down the bullies… but why didn’t he find out about this earlier?

Constantinos smirked at Pete’s reaction. “Gonna be a real show, huh? You better be there. Wouldn’t want to miss seeing your buddy Jimmy in action.”

He nodded numbly, still processing the information. “Yeah… I’ll be there. I will.”
